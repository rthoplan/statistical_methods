Understanding Errors in Data

Explore the two main types of statistical errors.

Sampling Error

The difference between a sample statistic and the true population parameter. It's the inherent uncertainty from not measuring everyone.

Key Characteristics:

  • Occurs only in sample surveys.
  • A natural result of randomness.
  • Can be reduced by increasing the sample size.

Non-Sampling Error

An error that occurs during data collection, causing the data to be incorrect. Increasing sample size will not fix these.

Non-Response

Scenario: An insurer sends a lifestyle survey to 1,000 policyholders. Only 300 respond, mostly older individuals.
Impact: The results will be biased, suggesting the portfolio is less healthy than it truly is, as the habits of younger, healthier policyholders are not captured.

Interviewer Bias

Scenario: An agent, wanting to secure a sale, asks an applicant, "You don't have any serious health conditions, right?"
Impact: This leading question pressures the applicant to say "no," even if they have a condition, leading to inaccurate underwriting data.

Coder Bias

Scenario: A data entry clerk is processing claim forms and consistently mistakes a doctor's handwritten "Smoker" for "Non-Smoker".
Impact: The resulting data will understate the number of claims from smokers, leading to incorrect mortality assumptions for that group.
